JUDGMENT of Ms. Justice O’Regan delivered on the 21st day of February, 2017

1. The applicant seeks leave to apply by way of judicial review to quash the decision of the respondent of 24th January, 2017 where the respondent determined that the applicant was not eligible for subsidiary protection.

2. The ex parte docket, grounding affidavit and statement of grounds in this matter are all dated 15th February, 2017. Submissions have also been filed bearing date 15th February, 2017.

3. The applicant came to Ireland on 8th September, 2013 and applied for asylum on 9th September, 2013. Having failed in his asylum application he made an application on 10th April, 2014 for subsidiary protection. This application was refused on 19th October, 2015. The applicant appealed on 7th December, 2015 and on 25th January, 2017 the decision of 19th October, 2015 was affirmed.

4. The decisions impugned runs to 18 pages.

5. The applicant complains that in assessing the concept of “real risk” the respondent required an excessive showing of likelihood instead of giving the term its plain and ordinary meaning. In legal argument in this regard reference is made to the definition of “real” which suggests that if there is at least some prospect of something happening then the risk is real and not artificial.

6. In Case C – 465/07 Elgafaji v. Staatssecretaris van Justite [2007] OJ C 008 the European Court of Justice discussed Art. 15(c) of the Qualification Directive, 2003 and stated that the definition in Art. 15(c) cover “a more general risk of harm” involving “an exceptional situation” characterised by “such a high degree of risk that substantial grounds would be shown for believing that the person would be subject individually to the risk in question.”

7. The suggestion therefore on behalf of the applicant that real risk is established if there is at least some prospect of something happening is not reasonable, arguable or weighty in all of the circumstances.
